Taurine and Atrial Fibrillation

Mechanisms, evidence and practical considerations

Why taurine?

Taurine is a sulfur‑containing amino acid involved in calcium handling, osmoregulation and membrane stabilization. In the context of AFib, proposed benefits include rhythm stability and protection against electrical and structural remodeling.

Mechanisms

  • Modulates intracellular calcium and potassium balance.
  • May reduce oxidative stress and inflammation.
  • Supports autonomic balance (sympatho‑vagal).
  • Membrane‑stabilizing properties (with magnesium synergy).

Evidence (Summary)

  • Preclinical AF models show shorter AF duration and less remodeling.
  • Case reports suggest reductions in ectopy; high‑quality AF RCTs are lacking.
  • See Research section for detailed citations.

Suggested dosing (informational)

Common supplemental ranges in studies and practice sources are 1–3 g/day in divided doses. Higher doses used in case reports should only be considered with medical supervision.

Safety: Generally well tolerated; potential GI discomfort at higher intakes. Interactions or contraindications may exist—always consult your physician.
