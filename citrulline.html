L‑Citrulline and Atrial Fibrillation

NO pathway support, evidence and practical considerations

Why L‑citrulline?

L‑citrulline is a precursor to L‑arginine and increases nitric‑oxide (NO) bioavailability. It may improve endothelial function and vascular tone—mechanisms relevant to AFib triggers and substrate.

Mechanisms

  • Raises plasma arginine and NO availability.
  • Improves endothelial function and blood flow.
  • Potential indirect effects on atrial substrate via vascular health.

Evidence (Summary)

  • Systematic reviews show improved NO/vascular function; direct AF outcomes are limited.
  • Human metabolomics show citrulline increases after successful ablation (association).
  • See Research section for citations and related NO donor trial.

Suggested dosing (informational)

Common supplemental ranges: 3–6 g/day, often split doses. May interact with antihypertensives or PDE‑5 inhibitors—consult your physician.
